Video Visits is a service that allows Kaiser Permanente patients to get faster access to care using a computer or mobile device video capabilities.

The research project was intended to help further streamline the Video Visits process. Video Visits is a complex product as it mixes service design, and interaction design in a variety of supported platforms.

At a Glance

Project Team

  • Lead Researcher: Me
  • Two additional research assistants

Goal of Project

  • Identify what factor(s) may be leading to unsuccessful Video Visits
  • Provide actionable recommendations to improve connection rates.

Outcomes

  • Two new features
  • Improved Video Visit onboarding
  • Findings became a key factor in renaming the mobile app from "KP Preventive Care" to "My Doctor Online"

Determining Research Scope

I met with the Video Visits team to discuss what they wanted to learn and what decisions needed to be informed. At this time, I also requested data logs to get an idea of what was happening with Video Visits from a quantitative perspective. From the discussions and the analysis of the data I learned...

Project Challenges

  • No clear consensus from the team about what parts of the process were problematic for users
  • Owners of specific parts of the service wanted to prioritize and validate their own hypothesis
  • Quantitative data was owned by different teams, and was not easily accessible.

Goal

We wanted to identify contributing factors that reduced Video Visit success rates. In order to increase the rate of connection, we chose to targeted three groups.

  • "Patient did not join": The clinician joined, but the patient did not.
  • "Neither joined": A scheduled Video Visit that neither the patient nor clinician joined.
  • "Joined but missed": A Video Visit where both parties connected to Video Visits, but were not on at the same time.

Developing Study Approach

The lack of consensus on where to focus the study led me to evaluate the entire service as a whole. My methods would have to allow me to identify potential pain points at any point in the experience. Then after finding some patterns, I could get more in-depth information on those specific problems from patients.

1st Method: Survey

A survey provided us easy access to a large pool of patients who had recently scheduled a Video Visit. I designed a survey with quantitative questions to test specific hypothesis with finite possibilities, and open-ended questions to identify potential pain points across the the Video Visits experience.

2nd Method: User Interviews

With the help of some colleagues, we conducted follow-up interviews with survey respondents. We were able to gather insights about a users experience from the point they scheduled an appointment, all the way to when they could not connect with their doctor.

Key Research Findings

  • Patients did not know where to access Video Visits.
  • Patients used the wrong Kaiser Permanente app due to similarities in the app name.
  • High response rates from patients whose doctor never arrived to their appointment (Using data logs, we confirmed that in many cases the doctor was simply late).

Results

  • New clinician-facing feature allowing them to notify patients of a late start
  • New email that gets sent before their appointment giving them direct access points to the web app or mobile app for Video Visits
  • Research became a key factor in renaming the "KP Preventive Care" app to "My Doctor Online"
